Healthcare · AI
Pulse
Product StrategyFHIR / EHRGPT-4User Research
The Problem

Physicians spend 30 to 60 minutes each day preparing for clinic visits, often during personal time. Patient information is scattered across lab results, imaging, medication histories, specialist notes, and external records. Chart fragmentation was rated the number one daily friction point in our research with 64 clinician survey responses and 22 interviews. 93% of physicians said they would feel better prepared if they had a consolidated view of their patients in advance.

Pulse converts fragmented EHR data into concise, patient-specific daily briefs delivered before clinic sessions. Each brief synthesizes what needs attention, what has changed since the last visit, and what to do next.

What I Learned

Through competitive analysis, we concluded Pulse does not currently represent a defensible standalone venture. Abridge ($250M Series D, 200+ health systems) and OpenEvidence (40% of U.S. physicians in under two years) have distribution and capital advantages a startup cannot replicate. The recommendation was to pursue partnership or licensing. This taught me that product-market fit alone is not enough. Timing, defensibility, and distribution matter just as much.

Consumer · AI · Commerce
Remy
Computer VisionPrompt EngineeringAffiliate Commerce
The Problem

American households waste $1,500 of food per year. 30 to 40% of purchased food gets thrown away because people forget what they have or don't know what to cook with it. Existing recipe apps start with the recipe and ask you to buy ingredients. Remy flips this: it starts with what you already have.

An AI app that scans your fridge via photo, identifies ingredients and brands, and generates personalized recipes. Includes a commerce layer linking users to Walmart for missing ingredients.

What I Learned

Prompt design is product design. Every line in the system prompt maps to a product requirement or business goal. I also learned that the best AI products hide the AI. I built confidence scores and prompt transparency panels, then removed them all because they made the app feel like a demo instead of a product.
